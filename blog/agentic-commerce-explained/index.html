Agentic Commerce Explained: How to Get Your Products Sold by AI Agents

Agentic commerce is when an AI agent, like ChatGPT or a shopping assistant, discovers, compares, and buys products on a customer's behalf. Instead of a person browsing your store, an AI does the browsing and hands you the order. It is moving from theory to revenue fast: AI-attributed orders on Shopify grew roughly 11x in a single year. If you sell online, your next customer might never see your homepage. Here is what agentic commerce is, why it matters now, and how to make sure your products are the ones the agents pick.

What is agentic commerce, in plain terms?

Traditional ecommerce assumes a human in the loop: they search, click, read, and check out. Agentic commerce removes most of those steps. A customer tells an AI assistant what they want ("a waterproof jacket under €150 that ships to Ireland by Friday"), and the agent finds candidates, compares them, and can complete the purchase. Your job shifts from convincing a person on a landing page to being legible and trustworthy to a machine that is shopping for them.

Why this matters right now

Two things make this urgent rather than futuristic. First, the volume is real and climbing: AI-driven orders are growing at a rate that very few channels ever do. Second, the major platforms are building the rails. Checkout inside AI assistants, agent-readable product feeds, and shopping APIs are shipping. Early movers are getting cited and recommended while competitors are still treating AI as a content gimmick. The brands that prepare now will own the default recommendations later.

How AI agents choose which products to recommend

Agents favour products that are easy to understand and easy to trust. In practice that means:

  • Clean, structured product data. Accurate titles, descriptions, prices, availability, shipping, and returns in machine-readable form (structured data and clean feeds), not buried in images or scripts.
  • Consistent information everywhere. If your price and stock say one thing on your site and another in a feed, the agent distrusts you. Consistency reads as reliability.
  • Third-party validation. Reviews, ratings, and mentions on independent sources. Agents weight outside evidence heavily because your own page is, by definition, promotional.
  • Clear answers to buying questions. Sizing, materials, compatibility, delivery times. The store that answers the question wins the recommendation.

How to prepare your Shopify store for agentic commerce

You do not need to rebuild anything. You need to become machine-legible. Start here: add and validate structured data for your products so agents can read price, availability, and specs without guessing. Keep your product feed clean and in sync with your live store. Write descriptions that answer real buying questions in plain language instead of marketing fluff. Build genuine reviews and get mentioned on the independent sites and communities your customers already trust. And make sure your store is indexed by Bing, not just Google, because several AI assistants retrieve through it.

The mistake most brands are about to make

Assuming agentic commerce is years away and doing nothing. The signal is already in the data. The brands that treat their product information as something a machine has to understand, today, will be the defaults that agents reach for tomorrow. The ones that wait will be invisible in a channel they did not see coming. Frequently asked questions

What is agentic commerce?
Agentic commerce is when an AI agent discovers, compares, and purchases products on a customer's behalf, instead of a human browsing and checking out manually. The customer states an intent and the agent does the shopping.

Is agentic commerce actually happening yet?
Yes. AI-attributed orders on Shopify grew roughly 11x in a year, and major platforms are shipping the checkout and feed infrastructure to support it. It is early but real.

How do I get my products recommended by ChatGPT or AI shopping agents?
Make your product data clean and structured, keep prices and stock consistent across your site and feeds, earn third-party reviews and mentions, answer real buying questions clearly, and ensure your store is indexed by Bing as well as Google.

Do I need to rebuild my Shopify store for agentic commerce?
No. The work is about making your existing store machine-legible: structured product data, clean feeds, clear answers, and external trust signals, not a redesign.
